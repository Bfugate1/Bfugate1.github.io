# Brian S. Fugate

**Associate Dean for Graduate Programs, Research, and Executive Education | Chair of Supply Chain Management**  
**University of Arkansas, Sam M. Walton College of Business**

Brian S. Fugate focuses on supply chain strategy, resilience, and knowledge-driven decision-making, with an emphasis on applied research, executive education, and industry collaboration across commercial and defense logistics contexts.

---

## Professional Overview

Brian S. Fugate serves the Sam M. Walton College of Business at the University of Arkansas as Associate Dean for Graduate Programs, Research, and Executive Education and as Chair of the Department of Supply Chain Management. His work spans graduate program leadership, corporate and defense-facing initiatives, and oversight of the Supply Chain Management Research Center and related consortium activities.

His research addresses strategic integration of demand and supply, knowledge flows, and sustainability, with particular focus on logistics performance, collaboration, and behavioral decision-making in supply chain management. He teaches across undergraduate, graduate, and executive levels, including experiential formats that connect academic concepts with practice in sectors such as food, retail, transportation, and government.

---

## Areas of Expertise

- Supply chain strategy and integration, including logistics leverage, supply chain orientation, and market-oriented logistics design
- Resilience, disruption, and uncertainty in global supply chains, including sustainable supply chain decision-making and collaboration under environmental and social pressures
- Knowledge flows and decision-making, with work linking knowledge management to operational and organizational performance and to logistics-based differentiation
- Defense and government-relevant logistics, including contested logistics concepts, coordination mechanisms, and applications relevant to innovation organizations such as the Defense Innovation Unit and Army Applications Laboratory
- Experiential and executive education design, including simulations, applied projects, and professional development for industry and government executives in supply chain management

---

## Industry and Government Engagement

Fugate collaborates extensively with industry partners through the Supply Chain Management Research Center at the Walton College, which connects faculty and students with companies seeking applied supply chain research and innovation. His work with corporate partners includes projects on logistics performance, sales and operations planning, demand forecasting, and sustainability, often translating empirical research into actionable frameworks and tools for practitioners.

His applied interests extend to government and defense-related logistics, including contested logistics and multi-echelon network resilience that are relevant to U.S. Department of Defense stakeholders. These activities intersect with innovation and experimentation communities such as the Defense Innovation Unit and the Army Applications Laboratory, where frameworks from his research on coordination, knowledge flows, and behavioral decision-making can inform logistics modernization and procurement.

## Editorial and Service Roles

Fugate serves as Associate Editor for the *Journal of Operations Management* and the *Journal of Business Logistics*. He has also served on the editorial board of the *Journal of Supply Chain Management* and has received multiple best reviewer recognitions in leading supply chain journals.

He previously served as Co-Editor-in-Chief of the *Journal of Supply Chain Management* from 2016 to 2020. He has contributed to professional service roles such as the SCPro Certification Committee for the Council of Supply Chain Management Professionals (CSCMP), in addition to program roles within academic and professional associations.
