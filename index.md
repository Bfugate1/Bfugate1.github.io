# Brian S. Fugate

**Associate Dean for Graduate Programs, Research, and Executive Education | Chair, J.B. Hunt Transport Department of Supply Chain Management**

**Oren Harris Endowed Chair in Transportation | Professor of Supply Chain Management**

**University of Arkansas, Sam M. Walton College of Business**

Brian S. Fugate focuses on supply chain strategy, resilience, and knowledge-driven decision-making, with an emphasis on applied research, executive education, and industry collaboration across commercial and defense logistics contexts.

***

## Professional Overview

Brian S. Fugate serves the Sam M. Walton College of Business at the University of Arkansas as Associate Dean for Graduate Programs, Research, and Executive Education and as Chair of the J.B. Hunt Transport Department of Supply Chain Management. He holds the Oren Harris Endowed Chair in Transportation and is Professor of Supply Chain Management. His work spans graduate program leadership, corporate and defense-facing initiatives, and oversight of the [Supply Chain Management Research Center](https://scmr.uark.edu) and related consortium activities.

His research addresses strategic integration of demand and supply, knowledge flows, and sustainability, with particular focus on logistics performance, collaboration, and behavioral decision-making in supply chain management. He teaches across undergraduate, graduate, and executive levels, including experiential formats that connect academic concepts with practice in sectors such as food, retail, transportation, and government.

Fugate previously served as a Fulbright U.S. Senior Researcher at the MIT-Zaragoza Logistics Center in Spain (2015) and held faculty positions at Colorado State University and Lehigh University. Prior to academia, he worked in industry with John Deere, Allied Signal, and Delta Airlines.

## Supply Chain Management Research Center

The [Supply Chain Management Research Center (SCMRC)](https://scmr.uark.edu) at the Walton College of Business serves as a hub connecting industry, faculty, and students. Founded in 1996 to link business practitioners with academic expertise, the center now:

- Facilitates collaborative research, events, and networking forums
- Hosts the Annual Trends in Supply Chain Management Conference, drawing professionals to explore current industry issues
- Runs the SCMRC Peer Learning Network for experiential peer-to-peer engagement
- Promotes best-practice exchange among corporate members and students
- Partners with organizations to deliver learning, professional development, and strategic insights

The center's broader mission is to advance supply chain knowledge, support student success, and cultivate future leaders by linking academic insight to industry practice.

## Teaching and Learning Innovation

Fugate champions experiential and active learning in supply chain education. His leadership of the department has strengthened:

- Industry-embedded projects
- Executive education initiatives
- Professional skill development
- Real-world case application in graduate and undergraduate coursework

These learning systems are designed to align academic outcomes with employer expectations and organizational needs.

Fugate co-developed patent-pending online teaching software (ACTIV82LRN) to bring active learning to online education, receiving the AACSB Innovations that Inspire Award in 2016. He has created over 30 instructional videos and implemented flipped/blended learning approaches across multiple courses.

## Industry, Defense, and Government Engagement

Fugate collaborates extensively with industry partners through the [Supply Chain Management Research Center](https://scmr.uark.edu) at the Walton College, which connects faculty and students with companies seeking applied supply chain research and innovation. His work with corporate partners includes projects on logistics performance, sales and operations planning, demand forecasting, and sustainability, often translating empirical research into actionable frameworks and tools for practitioners.

His applied interests extend to government and defense-related logistics, including contested logistics and multi-echelon network resilience that are relevant to U.S. Department of Defense stakeholders. These activities intersect with innovation and experimentation communities such as the Defense Innovation Unit and the Army Applications Laboratory, where frameworks from his research on coordination, knowledge flows, and behavioral decision-making can inform logistics modernization and procurement. This work addresses operational challenges in resource-constrained and disrupted environments, bridging academic research with defense applications.

Fugate's defense-related research spans supply chain plasticity during global disruptions, government customer concentration effects on firm performance, and the role of supplier network structures in government contracting outcomes.
